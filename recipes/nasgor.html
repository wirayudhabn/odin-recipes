Nasi Goreng

Nasi goreng is a flavorful Indonesian fried rice characterized by its smoky, savory, and sweet profile. Unlike other versions of fried rice, it relies heavily on kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), which caramelizes the grains to a deep golden brown. The base is typically a fragrant paste of shallots, garlic, and chilies, often enhanced with terasi (shrimp paste) for a bold umami kick. Traditionally considered a complete meal, it is almost always served with a fried egg on top, crunchy crackers (krupuk), and fresh slices of cucumber and tomato.

Main Ingredients (Bahan Utama)

  • Cold Cooked Rice: 2 plates (Nasi putih dingin, sebaiknya sisa semalam).
  • Kecap Manis (Sweet Soy Sauce): 2–3 tablespoons (Kecap manis).
  • Eggs: 1–2 pieces (Telur ayam).
  • Cooking Oil: 2 tablespoons (Minyak goreng).
  • Salt & Chicken Powder: To taste (Garam dan kaldu ayam bubuk secukupnya).

The Spice Paste (Bumbu Halus)

  • Shallots: 3 cloves (Bawang merah).
  • Garlic: 2 cloves (Bawang putih).
  • Red Chilies: 2–3 pieces (Cabai merah/rawit sesuai selera).
  • Shrimp Paste (Terasi): ½ teaspoon (Terasi bakar, opsional).

Condiments/Garnish (Pelengkap)

  • Fried Egg: Sunny side up (Telur mata sapi).
  • Cucumber & Tomato: Sliced (Irisan mentimun dan tomat).
  • Krupuk: Prawn crackers (Kerupuk udang).
  • Fried Shallots: For sprinkling (Bawang goreng untuk taburan).

Quick Recipe

  1. Sauté: Fry a paste of shallots, garlic, and chilies until fragrant.
  2. Scramble: Add an egg and scramble it directly in the pan.
  3. Mix: Toss in cold cooked rice and stir well.
  4. Season: Add sweet soy sauce (kecap manis), salt, and a pinch of chicken powder.
  5. Stir over high heat. Serve:Top with a fried egg and crackers.
